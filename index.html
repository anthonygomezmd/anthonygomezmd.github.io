I'm an MD with clinical experience spanning high-volume primary care in Mexico and U.S. observerships across neurology, internal medicine, and pediatrics. My foundation is structured clinical reasoning — the ability to take a complex, ambiguous patient presentation and work systematically toward a diagnosis.

That same skill is what makes me effective in medical AI training. I evaluate AI-generated responses the way I would evaluate a junior clinician's reasoning: checking for missed diagnoses, unsafe recommendations, guideline misalignment, and logical gaps that a non-clinician would never catch.

I've worked in resource-limited environments where you can't order every test, in medico-legal documentation where precision is non-negotiable, and in emergency triage where speed and accuracy have to coexist. That range of experience is what I bring to AI evaluation.

Currently preparing for the USMLE while actively building my practice at the intersection of medicine and AI.
